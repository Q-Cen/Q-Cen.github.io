My occupation is one where I manufacture the greens, specifically the trees. I like making trees because they're good for the environment. Taking care of them makes me feel good just as it would make the environment feel good, because it feels good to be doing something good. Now, about the process. I manufacture trees sometimes by planting, but in most cases I use a stick. It is pure irony that most my trees come from sticks, because sticks come from trees. Sometimes I wave the incantation, other times I merely put the sticks along with the bushes of leaves or pine I find and make the tree the good old way, inconvenient, but satisfying and soothing. The task of actually putting it together as opposed to doing it magically feels rewarding, because I know that once I've made the tree, the world will be better. There's also a bit of selfishness in my making trees, I will admit, because of my addiction to the color green. That's probably why I rarely make trees of any other color (though the cherry blossom looks quite lovely), but making trees isn't a bad thing at all, and that thought soothes me. I'm combatting the carbon emissions, I tell myself, so making trees is a noble thing. I don't think altruism is inherently selfish, but I don't consider what I do altruism. It's just something I love doing that happens ton help the environment.
